1525. Adipose tissue is a connective tissue found throughout the body.         It produces the hormone leptin in response to food intake.       Leptin increases the activity of anorexigenic neurons and decreases that of orexigenic neurons, producing a feeling of satiety after eating, thus affecting appetite and reducing the urge for further eating. 
1564. The fatty acids are released into the bloodstream for other tissues to use for ATP production.         The glucocorticoids primarily affect glucose metabolism by stimulating glucose synthesis.       Glucocorticoids also have anti-inflammatory properties through inhibition of the immune system.

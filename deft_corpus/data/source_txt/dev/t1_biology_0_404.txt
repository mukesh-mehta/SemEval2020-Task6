1328. Because most birds fly, their metabolic rates are high in order to efficiently process food and keep their body weight low.         The stomach of birds has two chambers: the proventriculus, where gastric juices are produced to digest the food before it enters the stomach, and the gizzard, where the food is stored, soaked, and mechanically ground.       The undigested material forms food pellets that are sometimes regurgitated.
